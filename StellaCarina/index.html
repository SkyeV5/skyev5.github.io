Deck room lists are ordered from the front of the ship to the back of the ship.

Need to know info
  • While in flight the ride is smooth enough that motion is barely felt if felt at all;
  • Under high turns and quick acceleration or deceleration, motion is felt normally. Better hold on!
  • Motion is felt normally on landing and take off.
  • Most people prone to motion sickness don't experiance it on the ship (but some still do)
Features common all areas on the ship have
  • A ceiling height of 7 feet
  • Grab rails
  • An intercom with speaker
Intercoms
  • A button to call the bridge or Captain's Quarters
  • A button to do an all-call to the entire ship
  • A button must be held down to speak
  • Sleeping Cabin intercoms have a toggle to mute all-calls, others cannot
  • Bridge has the master intercom that can call any individual intercom and do an all-call.
  • Both the Bridge and Captains Quarters intercoms can override all-call mutes, and toggle any intercoms ability to do all-calls
Windows
  • All windows have hard shades that be locked in place and block sunlight.
  • Most windows can be removed for emergency exits.
Corridors
  • 5ft wide

  • Engine room
  • Primary Manacrystal storage
  • Magical water supply and waste disposal system
  • Manacrystal fueled Electric Generator and Batteries, generator only kicks in when batteries are low
  • Manastream alignment system

  • Air Travel
  • Manastream alignment system that work in conjunction with deployable Manastream powered sails
    • When aligned to a Manastream, it's capable of travelling at supersonic speeds. However, the Manastreams are constantly shifting, and jumping from Manastream to Manastream during a long voyage is pretty much required.
  • Manacrystal fueled propulsion
    • The Manacrystal propulsion can attain speeds of around Mach 0.4, however it cannot sustain them indefinatly without refueling.
  • Passive levitation system
    • This is a seperate system from the others, and highly efficent. It only takes the highest quality of Manacrystals, but can operate for months straight if needed. For safety reasons, there are multiple isolated levitation systems across the ship, each with it's own power. If at least one of them is active, the ship can be lowered safely to the ground. It has failsafes to prevent being turned off in flight.
  • Seaworthy
  • Deployable regular wind based sails
    • Max speed of 10 knots, also usable in the air if needed.
  • Deployable propellers
    • Max speed of 50 knots
  • All passenger cabins have toggleable sound dampening walls
  • Landing Gear
    • Two large deployable and adjustable landing struts with wide feet on each side in the front of the ship, two in the rear of the ship, two adjacent to the ramps, and several smaller ones along the bottom. Reference Image
  • Shielding System
    • Protects from projectiles and dampens impacts, as well as mitigating high winds and most weather on the open deck while in flight. Only projectile protection functions when in seafaring mode.
  • Auto-pilot
    • Only able to keep the ship going in a specific compass direction when not aligned to a Manastream. Manual is not available when aligned to and travelling on a Manastream and will travel on it for the distance selected when alignment was activated or when manually stopped, then shift into levitation only mode until the helm is taken
  • Inertia inhibitor
    • Almost entirely negates the effect of movement on the inside and top deck. Primarily inhibits forward movement, acceleration, speed changes, and low angle turns. Not as effective on sharp turns, abrupt stops, or tilting. Doesn't function when in seafaring mode.
